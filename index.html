Russians have been cheating the 1987 nuclear treaty for years

Us intelligence first suspected Moscow of developing a ground-based nuclear-armed cruise missile in breach of the Intermediate-Range Nuclear Forces treaty ten years ago. The discovery provoked a confrontation with the Russians that endured during the Obama administration and generated growing alarm in Nato as the new weapon would directly threaten Europe. It was only last year that the US put a name to the missile in question: the land-based Novator 9M729 (designated by Nato as SSC-8), a cruise missile that western intelligence agencies insisted had a range that breached the INF treaty. The 1987 treaty, which eliminated a class of nuclear missiles, set range limits of between 500km (310 miles) and 5,500km (3,418 miles).

World’s oldest shipwreck is discovered in Black Sea

The world’s oldest intact shipwreck, complete with mast, rudders and rowing benches, has been found at the bottom of the Black Sea where it has been lying for more than 2,400 years. The 23-metre merchant vessel was found lying on its side by a remote-controlled submarine.

Gambler who stole €1.7m says bookies must stop playing dumb

A worker who stole €1.75 million from An Post to feed his betting habit has said that not enough is being done to check where addicted gamblers are getting their funds. Tony O’Reilly, from Co Carlow, was jailed for four years with one year suspended in late 2012. His Paddy Power account, under the name Tony 10, had a turnover of €10 million. He is now working as an addiction counsellor with a company that aims to reduce the harm caused by gambling. O’Reilly said that bookmakers were not protecting their customers and that there were not enough checks against money laundering.
